Motion Sickness Modeling & Control of Autonomous Vehicles

Project information

  • Category: Vehicle Dynamics, MATLAB Simulink, CarSim
  • Project date: April 2022
  • Project URL: github.com/IshanVyas0610/Motion-Sickness-Modeling-and-Control-of-AVs

  • The study focused on Motion Sickness Dose Value (MSDV) and how it is affected by a vehicle's lateral, longitudinal, and yaw acceleration in different road scenarios. MSDV is a measure of motion sickness that takes into account the intensity and duration of the motion stimulus. The study aimed to evaluate how different driving conditions affect MSDV, specifically focusing on the role of yaw acceleration.
  • The study discovered that yaw rate has a significant impact on MSDV. Yaw acceleration is the rotational motion around the vertical axis, and it is affected by factors such as vehicle speed, steering angle, and road conditions. The study found that higher yaw rates lead to higher MSDV values, indicating a higher risk of motion sickness.
  • Based on the findings of the study, a Simulink model was developed to minimize yaw rate through Direct Yaw control. Direct Yaw Control (DYC) is a vehicle stability control system that uses differential braking to control the vehicle's yaw rate. The Simulink model was designed to optimize DYC to minimize the vehicle's yaw rate and, therefore, reduce MSDV.
